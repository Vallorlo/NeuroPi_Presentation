🧠 BCI Paradigms Evaluated

Motor Imagery

Excellent

Principle: Imagining movement activates motor cortex without physical movement, producing Event-Related Desynchronization (ERD) in mu (8-12 Hz) and beta (13-30 Hz) frequency bands with clear lateralization patterns.

Implementation Details:
  • Tasks: Left/Right Hand, Feet, Rest
  • Features: CSP feature extraction
  • Classifier: ATCNet architecture
  • Channels: C3, C4, Cz (motor cortex)

P300 Paradigm

Excellent

Principle: Positive EEG deflection occurring ~300ms after recognizing a target stimulus in an oddball paradigm with flashing symbols in matrix interface.

Implementation Details:
  • Signal: Strong peak at Pz/Cz
  • Processing: Time-locked averaging
  • Classifier: CNN-LSTM-Attention
  • Interface: Matrix flashing paradigm

Speech Decoding

Poor

Objective: Attempted to decode speech from brain signals but faced high contamination from facial EMG and inconsistent results with consumer EEG hardware.

Challenges Encountered:
  • Issue: High muscle interference
  • Limitation: Low spatial resolution
  • Result: Unreliable for real-time
  • Conclusion: Not feasible for consumer EEG

🔬 Data Collection & Processing Pipeline

Equipment & Protocols

Hardware: Emotiv EPOC+ (14-channel, 128 Hz, saline electrodes)
Paradigm: Visual cues for structured trials
Sessions: Multi-session recordings for robustness
Data Format: CSV annotation with timestamps and channel data

Preprocessing & Features

Filtering: Bandpass filtering (8-30 Hz), Notch at 50 Hz
Artifacts: ICA and statistical thresholding for artifacts
Features: CSP, PSD, and Wavelet Transform features
Validation: Cross-validation training strategy
